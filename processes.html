Strategic Security Processes

High-maturity operational workflows for Zero Trust and SecOps.

ðŸ”„ Zero Trust Implementation Lifecycle

Zero Trust is a journey, not a destination. It follows a continuous improvement loop.

1. Visualize & Define

Identify the Protect Surface (DAAS: Data, Applications, Assets, Services). Map the transaction flows across the network.

2. Architect & Policy

Design a Zero Trust architecture tailored to the environment. Write "Policy as Code" (Rego) to enforce granular access.

3. Monitor & Maintain

Continuously inspect and log all traffic. Use AI/ML to detect anomalies in user behavior (UEBA).

ðŸš¨ Incident Response (Assume Breach)

In Zero Trust, we shift from "Prevention" to "Detection & Rapid Containment."

# 1. Preparation: Hardened identity, EDR coverage, and SOAR playbooks. # 2. Identification: Correlating telemetry from Identity, Device, and Network. # 3. Containment: Micro-segmentation to isolate the compromised segment instantly. # 4. Eradication: Re-provisioning compromised assets (Immutable Infrastructure). # 5. Recovery: Restoring from validated, secure backups. # 6. Lessons Learned: Updating policies to prevent lateral movement.

ðŸ†” Identity Lifecycle & Governance

Moving from static accounts to dynamic, risk-based access.

  • JIT (Just-In-Time): Granting access only for a specific duration.
  • JEA (Just-Enough-Access): Restricting permissions to the absolute minimum required for the task.
  • SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management): Automating user provisioning and de-provisioning.
  • Recertification: Automated monthly review of "High Privilege" accounts.

BAS (Breach & Attack Simulation)

Automated execution of MITRE ATT&CK techniques against your defenses to find gaps in real-time. This replaces the "snapshot in time" approach with continuous assurance.
